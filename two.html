How ISP works during rush hours?

Internet peak hours refer to the times when most users are online at the same time. In the UK, this period usually occurs between 7 pm and 11 pm. Users usually experience slowdowns when browsing or downloading content at this time. This type of peak network congestion is similar to traffic rush hour, where resource demands exceed the network's carrying capacity.
Compared with 2011 data, Google's 2017 analysis report showed that daily network usage peaked from 9 a.m. to noon, then gradually decreased until a slight recovery from 7 p.m. to 10 p.m. And it drops to its lowest at 4am.
